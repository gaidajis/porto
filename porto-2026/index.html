Home Base

Torel Saboaria.

A single address for the whole week. A nineteenth-century palace on a quiet Porto hilltop — gardens, pools, a Michelin-starred table. No packing, no rushing. Slow mornings, long lunches, napping by the pool.

Rua de Sá de Noronha · Porto

Torel Saboaria

"A nineteenth-century palace, reborn as Porto's most elegant family retreat."

Intimate and refined — thirty-two rooms set within a restored neoclassical manor, wrapped by shaded gardens. The outdoor pool catches the late-afternoon light; the indoor spa pool is a sanctuary on cooler mornings. Dinner is served at Torel 1884, the property's Michelin-starred restaurant, where Chef Júlio Pereira reinterprets Portuguese cuisine with quiet brilliance. Cradles, high chairs and babysitting are arranged without fuss.

Ribeira, the bridge, port cellars.

Porto → Vila Nova de Gaia
Ribeira district Dom Luís I Bridge Cálem Cellars
Golden hour

The Douro, at sunset.

A fifteen-minute stroll from the hotel leads to the Ribeira — a cascade of colourful houses tumbling toward the river. Cross the Dom Luís I Bridge on its lower deck (stroller-friendly), and emerge in Vila Nova de Gaia. Alternate port tastings at Cálem. End with a rabelo boat cruise — fifty minutes on the Douro beneath the six bridges.

Fri · 31 July

The Douro, by train.

Porto → Pinhão → Porto
São Bento station Pinhão UNESCO Douro Valley
Signature day

Vineyards, terraced in time.

From São Bento station — its walls wrapped in twenty thousand azulejo tiles — board the regional train to Pinhão. Two hours each way through the oldest demarcated wine region in the world, a UNESCO landscape of terraced vines tumbling toward the river. In Pinhão: a gentle riverside walk, lunch at a quinta, the rhythm of the train lulling you for a nap on the return.

A slow beach day

Where river meets the sea.

Metro line A — twenty minutes from Bolhão, near the hotel — to Matosinhos. A wide, calm Atlantic beach, suitable for toddlers. Morning: sand play, soft waves, a beachside café. Lunch: fresh grilled fish at a traditional marisqueira on the shore. Afternoon stroll south to Foz do Douro, where the river meets the ocean. Gelato stop.

V
Sun · 02 August

Aveiro — Venice of Portugal.

Aveiro · Costa Nova
Aveiro Costa Nova Art Nouveau Museum
New destination

Moliceiros, painted & gliding.

One hour south by train from São Bento. Aveiro — Portugal's "Venice" — is a city of canals crossed by colourful moliceiro boats, hand-painted with folk motifs. Morning: a gentle moliceiro ride, the kindest boat trip. Art Nouveau facades glide past. Afternoon: a short bus ride to Costa Nova to see the famous striped palheiros, and a taste of ovos moles — Aveiro's signature yolk-and-sugar sweet shaped like shells and fish.

Lello, gardens, a galão.

Porto · City centre
Livraria Lello Palácio de Cristal Manteigaria Café Majestic
The slowest day

The bookstore that inspired Potter.

A morning at Livraria Lello — book ahead; ten minutes' walk from the hotel. The red staircase, the stained-glass ceiling, the scent of old paper. Then Jardins do Palácio de Cristal — peacocks, river views. Afternoon: café-hopping — a warm pastel at Manteigaria, a galão under the Art Nouveau ceiling of Café Majestic. Sunset from the Mirante da Vitória.

The market, the last stroll.

Porto · Bolhão → Ribeira

Farewell day

Markets, azulejos, a last francesinha.

Morning at Mercado do Bolhão — five minutes from the hotel, beautifully restored. Portuguese ceramics, cork souvenirs, aged cheeses, a bottle or two of port to carry home. Lunch: the francesinha at Café Santiago, Porto's iconic layered sandwich. Afternoon: one last meander through Ribeira, azulejo tiles tucked into a suitcase. Evening: farewell dinner beneath the palace chandeliers of Torel 1884.
